Introduction¶

This dataset, sourced from The Movie Database (TMDb), provides a comprehensive look into 10,000 movies, covering essential attributes such as user ratings, revenue, budget, cast, and genres. this dataset offers a treasure trove of insights.

Dataset Description¶

  1. id: An integer value serving as a unique identifier for each entry in the dataset.
  2. imdb_id: A unique identifier provided by IMDb for each movie.
  3. popularity: A float value indicating the popularity score of the movie.
  4. budget: An integer value representing the budget of the movie.
  5. revenue: An integer value representing the revenue generated by the movie.
  6. original_title: The original title of the movie.
  7. cast: Names of the main cast members of the movie.
  8. homepage: The URL of the movie's official website, if available.
  9. director: The name of the director of the movie.
  10. tagline: A short memorable phrase associated with the movie, often used in marketing.
  11. keywords: Keywords or phrases associated with the movie for indexing and searching purposes.
  12. overview: A brief summary or description of the movie.
  13. runtime: The duration of the movie in minutes.
  14. genres: Categories or genres that the movie belongs to.
  15. production_companies: Names of the production companies involved in making the movie.
  16. release_date: The date when the movie was released.
  17. vote_count: The count of votes given to the movie.
  18. vote_average: The average rating given to the movie.
  19. release_year: The year when the movie was released.
  20. budget_adj: The budget of the movie adjusted for inflation.
  21. revenue_adj: The revenue generated by the movie adjusted for inflation.

Question(s) for Analysis¶

  1. Is there a relation between budget and revenue?
  2. Is there a relation between budget and popularity?

  5. Who is the top 10 movies by revenue?

def bar_plot(data, x, y, title, x_label, y_label):
    '''
    Function to create a bar plot.
    input:
        data: pandas dataframe
        x: x-axis variable
        y: y-axis variable
        title: plot title
        x_label: x-axis label
        y_label: y-axis label
    '''
    plt.figure(figsize=(6, 4)) # Setting the figure size
    sns.barplot(x=x, y=y, data=data) 
    plt.title(title) # Setting the plot title
    plt.xlabel(x_label) # Setting the x-axis label
    plt.ylabel(y_label) # Setting the y-axis label
